# Educational Technology Plan

Provide a brief description of the purpose of your educational technology Plan

## Context

### Course

Introduction to Computer Science - 9/10th grade 

### Situation
This is an imaginary class based on the idea that I would continue teaching in the same district I am currently in but at the high school level. I am going to use as the constraints, the constraints at my current school in order to be able to work off of what I know. 

All students will have Chromebooks assigned by the school. Some students will not have their Chromebooks because they are lost and/or broken and in the process of being replaced. I will have access to a cart of Chromebooks for students to use. Budget will be low so I will want to get as much out of these Chromebooks as possible through the use of mostly cloud applications/programming. This will limit hardware used in the class. 

My experience is that it is difficult to get the district to approve the use of new paid software in the class, for this reason I will also be trying my best to use freeware. 

Additionally based on the demographics of the student bodies in our district, there will be a varied degree of English ability in the class, including students who have recently arrived from Latin America, especially Central America. 

## Plan

### Software Specifications
We will start the class working with make code and then transition into working with javascript in the browser version of the VScode IDE with github. I will also use codeworkout as an extra resource for practice problems. I will do grading of the big projects by hand and for the small in class projects I will use peer-check/code review to make sure that the outputs work. 

Grades will be submitted through our schools licensed software, StudentVue/ParentVue/Synergy. Course information will be available through Google Classroom. 

### Hardware Specifications
The main hardware students will have access to in the class is the Chromebooks that they are given by the district/school. I am not sure what the exact specs of each Chromebook are (it varies by school) and I suspect that they all have varying specs, manufacturers and models. Based on my own experience at my school the most popular one is the Lenovo 500e which has a 1.1 GHz Intel processor, with integrated RAM (4-8gb) and graphics. It also has 32-64gb storage.  Needless to say its not a particularly powerful computer and its capabilities are further limited by the Chrome OS. Thus, I will stick to mostly cloud tools for this course. 

### Application
As this is an introductory course with the goal of getting students' feet wet with Computer Science and programming, I will not be assigning homework in the class. The goal will be to engage students in in-class work, and not load them down with homework when the goal of the class is to pique their interest in the field. 

This means the grade for the course will be largely based on in-class project-based instruction. I might be biased because this is my comfort zone when it comes to teaching practices, but I also believe it will be the most effective approach based on education research and this type of course. 

We will start each class with a practice problem (multiple choice on the board with mentimeter)

Then short (10-15 min) instruction that introduces a new concept (like for loops)

Then try it/play with it period where students play around with this new concept on their own either in make code or their IDE. I will have a github repo that they can fork to get them straight into this with a multi-tier goal for them to work on (i.e. "make this for loop print 'potato' 50 times" etc.). The goal of this is to just get them playing with the new concept. 

Then into assigned and rotating groups to work on a class assignment for the rest of the period. The class assignment is a more formal coding challenge. It will be presented in a way where I am pushing them to use team design-thinking and iteration principles. (ie "How do you best solve this problem?" "Why is this code not working the way you want?")

Every few weeks we will have a big project where students make their own groups and work on a more fun/creative/self directed project (ie "using functions, make a game in make code" or "make a web app about anything of your choice that takes user input and uses it to produce some output")

### Impacts
I would say that the biggest challenges that I face in terms of making sure that this class is accessible for ALL students come from the challenges of making the topic interesting or engaging for students, and the difficulty of teaching to a multi-lingual class. 

When it comes to making the topic interesting, I want to focus the class on practice and playing around with concepts. The main way I will do this is through project-based learning and the design process. By having students implement their own ideas for their projects, hopefully they will be more engaged as they can bring in their own interests to the subject. 

When it comes to linguistic issues, a major challenge that I face is that very few educational resources that I could find when it came to computer science were available either in multiple languages, or in Spanish at all. This means that most of the work on this front will need to come from me as an educator. I can help on this front through both explicit classroom practices as well as broader pedagogical decisions. When it comes to explicit practices, I will try my best to make sure assignments are available in spanish and that there are translations on the slide, along with graphics and images to help explain concepts. I can also use more explicit examples and practice activities (like a version of the peanut butter and jelly activity that focuses less on intricacies of language for example). When it comes to broader pedagogy I need to use practices that research shows are critical for education of English Language Learners. For example, I want to integrate group work deeply into this class. Not only is group work core to the practice of computer science in the field, but it is also critical for bridging language divides in the classroom.
